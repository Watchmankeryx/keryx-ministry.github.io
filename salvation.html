The Gospel in 4 Points

1. YOU ARE A SINNER

Romans 3:23 "For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God"

We've all broken God's laws. We've all lied, stolen, lusted, disobeyed, and put ourselves above God. Every human being is a sinner, and sin separates us from a holy God.

2. SIN'S PENALTY IS DEATH

Romans 6:23 "For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord"

3. CHRIST PAID YOUR PENALTY

Romans 5:8 "But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us"

Jesus Christ‚ÄîGod in human flesh‚Äîcame to earth, lived a perfect life, and died on the cross. He took YOUR punishment for YOUR sins. He died the death you deserved. Three days later, He rose from the dead, proving He has power over sin, death, and hell.

4. YOU MUST BELIEVE AND RECEIVE

John 1:12 "But as many as received Him, to them He gave the right to become children of God, to those who believe in His name"
Romans 10:9-10 "If you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved. For with the heart one believes unto righteousness, and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation"
Ephesians 2:8-9 "For by grace you have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God, not of works, lest anyone should boast"

Salvation is a free gift that cannot be earned. You cannot work your way to heaven. You cannot be good enough. You must simply believe in Jesus Christ and receive Him as your Lord and Savior by faith.

Can I lose my salvation?

No. Jesus said: "I give them eternal life, and they shall never perish; neither shall anyone snatch them out of My hand" (John 10:28). Once you're saved, you're saved forever. God keeps you, not you keeping yourself.

What if I sin after being saved?

1 John 1:9: "If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness." Believers still sin, but we confess and God forgives. Sin doesn't cancel salvation‚Äîit damages fellowship with God, which is restored through confession.

1 John 5:13: "These things I have written to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may KNOW that you have eternal life." God wants you to have assurance! If you genuinely believed in Jesus Christ and received Him as Lord, you ARE saved. Period.

I don't feel different. Am I really saved?

Salvation is based on FACT (God's promise), not FEELING. Your emotions may or may not change immediately, but God's Word never changes. Romans 10:13 promises: "Whoever calls on the name of the Lord shall be saved." If you called, you ARE saved‚Äîregardless of how you feel.
